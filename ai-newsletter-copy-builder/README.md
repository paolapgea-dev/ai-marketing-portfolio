# AI Newsletter Strategy Engine

A data-informed workflow that generates newsletter concepts, structures, subject lines, and copy based on behavioral science principles and patterns discovered from real newsletter performance data.

---

## Overview

I built this workflow after manually analyzing newsletter performance over multiple months.

The goal was not to automate newsletter writing, but to identify repeatable patterns behind high-performing newsletters and use AI to accelerate idea generation, subject line development, newsletter structure creation, and drafting while keeping human editorial judgment at the center.

---

## The Problem

Writing a weekly newsletter that's consistently high quality is difficult.

The biggest challenges are finding compelling angles, strong subject lines, and useful structures often takes longer than drafting the content.

---

## Research & Discovery

I launched an experimental rebranding of the Talaera newsletter, making it about 15 Tiny Lessons that land on your inbox every Tuesday to helo you stand out in meetings, sound more confident, and grow in your career.

Before building the workflow, I manually created the newsletters and reviewed newsletter performance data and editorial decisions across 10+ newsletter editions.

The analysis focused on:

* Open rates
* Subject line patterns
* Newsletter themes
* Editorial angles
* Reader psychology

Several patterns emerged.

### Patterns That Performed Well

* Identity-threat framing
* Status-related framing
* Real-time workplace moments
* Situational communication challenges
* Specific professional scenarios

Examples:

* "Stay sharp when meetings move fast"
* "Stay in control"
* "Do your words match your ambitions?"
* "What to say when someone asks what you do"

### Patterns That Underperformed

* Broad communication topics
* Generic skill-building language
* Abstract concepts without a concrete scenario

Examples:

* "Make people listen to you"
* "People who think differently"

---

## The Solution

I built an AI workflow that:

1. Generates newsletter concepts
2. Suggests editorial angles
3. Creates newsletter structures
4. Generates and scores subject lines
5. Drafts newsletter intros
6. Produces a complete draft

while keeping a human editor in control of all final decisions.

## Decision Framework

The workflow is built on a decision framework derived from historical newsletter performance rather than generic AI prompting.

As new newsletters are published, performance data can be reviewed and used to refine the underlying patterns, heuristics, and evaluation criteria that guide idea generation and subject line recommendations.

## What I Learned

* High-performing newsletters are usually driven by framing, not topic.
* Specific workplace moments outperform broad communication themes.
* Editorial systems are more valuable than one-off prompts.
* AI works best when guided by proven patterns rather than creativity alone.

---

## Outcome

The workflow accelerates newsletter ideation and drafting while preserving human editorial control and maintaining alignment with proven audience engagement patterns.

---
## What Makes This Interesting

This workflow was not built by starting with AI. It was built by first identifying patterns in newsletter performance, then encoding those patterns into a repeatable decision framework. 

The AI layer accelerates execution. The underlying value comes from the research, experimentation, and behavioral insights behind the workflow.
